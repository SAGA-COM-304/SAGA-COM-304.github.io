Introduction

TL;DR

  • Minimalist any‑to‑any multimodal model for text, image, audio & video.
  • Extensions add audio & video modalities
  • Create a new dataset from VggSound dataset (youtube videos) to combine audio and video
  • Used depth-to-anything to convert rgb to depth image / mimi tokenizer for audio and Cosmos Tokenizer for audio / video

The nano4M project, developed for the COM-304 course at EPFL, is a minimalist multimodal model inspired by 4M. It excels in diverse tasks, supports fine-tuning for specialized applications, and enables flexible any‑to‑any multimodal generation.

Our work includes a causal Transformer for text generation, an encoder-only Transformer for image generation, and a multimodal encoder-decoder for tasks like in-painting and cross-modal generation. We enhanced nano4M with innovative extensions to push its capabilities further.

Model Capabilities

Text Generation

A decoder-only Transformer enables autoregressive text generation, trained on a simple dataset to produce coherent text sequences.

Image Generation

An encoder-only Transformer supports autoregressive and masked image generation, enabling tasks like 이미지 completion.

Multimodal Generation

An encoder-decoder model enables any‑to‑any generation, supporting chained multimodal outputs across text and images.

Implemented Extensions

Implemented Audio and Video modalities

Extended nano4M to generate audios and videos

Super‑Resolution

Trained nano4M to map low‑resolution (224×224) images to high‑resolution (448×448), enhancing output detail.

Classifier‑Free Guidance

Implemented classifier‑free guidance by dropping conditioning during training, improving generation quality with adjustable scales.

Conclusion

The nano4M project successfully implemented a minimalist multimodal model capable of text, image, and cross‑modal generation. Our extensions—span‑masking, super‑resolution, and classifier‑free guidance—significantly improved performance across various tasks. Limitations include computational constraints and challenges with complex datasets. Future work could explore scaling laws or additional modalities like audio.
